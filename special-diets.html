Special Diets & Sustainable Shopping in Sweden

Your guide to halal, kosher, paleo, and zero-waste shopping across Sweden

Understanding Special Diets

Halal Food

What is Halal?

Halal means "permissible" in Arabic and refers to food that complies with Islamic dietary laws. Key requirements include:

  • No pork or pork products
  • No alcohol or alcohol-derived ingredients
  • Meat must be slaughtered according to Islamic principles
  • No blood products
  • Gelatin must be from halal sources

Cost Impact: Halal meat is often 10-15% more expensive than conventional, but bulk buying and sales can help reduce costs.

Kosher Food

What is Kosher?

Kosher refers to food that complies with Jewish dietary laws (kashrut). Key requirements include:

  • No mixing of meat and dairy products
  • No pork, shellfish, or other non-kosher animals
  • Meat must be slaughtered and prepared according to Jewish law
  • Separate utensils and preparation areas for meat and dairy
  • Certified supervision required

Cost Impact: Kosher products can be 20-30% more expensive, but many basic ingredients are naturally kosher.

Paleo Diet

What is Paleo?

The Paleolithic diet focuses on foods available to early humans. Includes:

  • Meat, fish, and poultry
  • Vegetables and fruits
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Healthy oils (olive, coconut, avocado)

Excludes: Grains, legumes, dairy, processed foods, sugar, potatoes

Cost Impact: Can be 30-50% more expensive due to emphasis on organic, grass-fed products, but seasonal shopping helps.

Zero Waste Living

What is Zero Waste?

A lifestyle that aims to eliminate waste by following the 5 R's:

  • Refuse: Items you don't need
  • Reduce: What you consume
  • Reuse: Items multiple times
  • Recycle: When necessary
  • Rot: Compost organic waste

Cost Impact: Often saves 20-40% on grocery bills through bulk buying, reduced packaging, and less food waste.

Gluten-Free Diet

What is Gluten-Free?

Essential for people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Avoids:

  • Wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives
  • Most breads, pasta, and baked goods
  • Many processed foods with hidden gluten
  • Soy sauce, some seasonings

Safe foods: Rice, potatoes, quinoa, certified gluten-free oats, meat, fish, vegetables, fruits

Cost Impact: 30-50% more expensive, but focus on naturally gluten-free whole foods helps reduce costs.

Lactose-Free Diet

What is Lactose-Free?

For people with lactose intolerance who can't digest milk sugar. Avoids:

  • Regular milk, cream, ice cream
  • Most cheeses (except aged hard cheeses)
  • Butter (small amounts often tolerated)
  • Hidden lactose in processed foods

Alternatives: Lactose-free milk, plant-based milks, lactase supplements

Cost Impact: 15-25% more expensive, but Swedish stores have good selection and competitive prices.
