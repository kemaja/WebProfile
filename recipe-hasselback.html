Hasselbackspotatis (Swedish Sliced Baked Potatoes)

Cost per serving: ~11 kr
Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 88 medium potatoes (Almond or King Edward) - 12 kr (Lidl)
  • 50g1.8 oz butter - 10 kr (Lidl Milbona)
  • 2 tbsp2 tbsp olive oil - 6 kr (ICA Basic)
  • 1 tsp1 tsp sea salt - 1 kr
  • 1/2 tsp1/2 tsp black pepper - 1 kr
  • 2 cloves2 cloves garlic, minced (optional) - 2 kr (Lidl)
  • 2 tbsp2 tbsp fresh herbs or 1 tsp dried (rosemary, thyme) - 8 kr (fresh herbs, or 2 kr dried)

Optional toppings:

  • 50g1.8 oz grated cheese - 8 kr (any Swedish cheese)
  • 2 tbsp2 tbsp breadcrumbs - 2 kr (make from old bread)
Total cost: ~44 kr for 4 servings (11 kr per serving)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 220¬∞C (425¬∞F).
  2. Wash and scrub potatoes thoroughly but leave skin on.
  3. Place each potato on a cutting board. Make cuts 3-5mm1/8 inch apart, cutting about 3/4 through the potato (not all the way through).
  4. Pro tip: Place wooden spoons on either side of the potato to prevent cutting all the way through.
  5. Place sliced potatoes in a baking dish.
  6. Melt butter and mix with olive oil, minced garlic, salt, pepper, and herbs.
  7. Brush the butter mixture generously over each potato, making sure to get between the slices.
  8. Bake for 30 minutes.
  9. Remove and brush with more butter mixture. If using cheese and breadcrumbs, sprinkle on now.
  10. Bake another 15-20 minutes until golden brown and crispy on the edges.
  11. The slices should fan out and the edges should be crispy while the inside remains fluffy.
  12. Serve immediately while hot and crispy.

About This Swedish Delicacy

Hasselbackspotatis was invented in the 1940s at Hasselbacken restaurant on Djurg√•rden island in Stockholm. Named after the restaurant, these accordion-style potatoes quickly became a staple of Swedish cuisine and are now served worldwide.

The beauty of hasselback potatoes lies in their unique texture - crispy, golden edges with a fluffy, tender interior. They're perfect alongside Swedish meatballs, fish dishes, or as part of a traditional Swedish Christmas spread.

Swedish serving tradition: These are often served during holidays and special occasions, particularly at Christmas alongside ham, herring, and other traditional dishes.

Pro technique: The key is getting the butter and seasonings down between the slices. Use a pastry brush or spoon to really work the mixture into all the cuts.

Money-saving tip: Buy potatoes in bulk bags from Lidl - they're always cheapest there. Choose medium-sized, uniform potatoes for the best presentation.
